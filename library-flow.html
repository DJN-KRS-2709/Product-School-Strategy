Living Content Library — Operational Flow

How signals become atoms, atoms become courses, and courses enrich the library

Stage 1
Signal Intake
Raw information flows in from 10 channels — 5 internal, 5 external. Each carries different volume, value, and cadence.
Internal (5)

Student surveys, Customer Success, instructor observations, enterprise demand, cohort transcripts

External (5)

Lenny's newsletter & podcast, Product School events, LinkedIn & social, Substack & blogs, Reddit communities

📡
Stage 2
Quality Gates
Not everything enters the library. Every signal passes through four sequential gates before an atom is created.
1. Extraction
2. Source Evaluation
3. Relevance Check
4. Novelty Filter
"What is the core insight? Is the source credible? Which courses benefit? Is it already covered?"
🔍
Stage 3
Atom Creation & Typing
Signals that pass the gates are structured into one of four atom types, each with full metadata: unique ID, source, dates, confidence level, tags, and course mappings.
◆ Concept

Canonical definitions

▲ Framework

Reusable models

● Evidence

Case studies & stats

⚡ Signal

Emerging insights

⚛
Stage 4
Confidence Lifecycle
Every atom enters at a confidence level and is promoted or deprecated through curation. This is the trust contract.
Speculative

Signal only. Trending or unverified. Do not use in courses without escalation.

Emerging

Single credible source, not yet classroom-tested. Use with context: "recent research suggests..."

Validated

Multiple sources, taught in cohort, positive student signal. Use directly and cite as established.

Speculative Validated
🎯
Stage 5
Freshness Monitoring & Lint
Every atom has a verification SLA based on its type. Automated alerts fire when atoms exceed their window. Periodic lint checks catch contradictions, orphans, and gaps.
◆ Concept Every 180 days
▲ Framework Every 180 days
● Evidence Every 90 days
⚡ Signal Every 60 days
Contradiction check
Orphan detection
Gap analysis
Stale claim sweep
🔄
Stage 6
Output Composition
Courses and other outputs don't store content — they reference atoms. A single atom can serve multiple outputs simultaneously. Update once, propagate everywhere.
Certifications

9 courses compose from shared atoms. Cross-course consistency is structural.

Enterprise

Custom workshops composed from verified atoms with freshness metadata for each element.

Thought Leadership

Podcasts, blogs, LinkedIn, speaker materials, newsletters — all sourced from the library.

Average target: 2.5+ courses per atom — every atom earns its place through cross-course utility.
📦
Stage 7
Governance & Roles
Clear ownership at every stage ensures quality without bottlenecks. Low barrier to create, high bar to validate.
Create → Anyone

Course authors, instructors, content team, CPTO. Capture signal from everywhere.

Promote → Content leads

Source verification, peer review, course mapping decision required.

Deprecate → CPTO + leads

Archived with full history and rationale. Never deleted.

Continuous Feedback Loop — Back to Stage 1

Every output generates signal that flows back into the library. Student confusion flags atoms for review. Instructor adaptations enrich teaching notes. Enterprise demand shapes investment priority. The system compounds — it gets smarter with every cohort, every delivery, every interaction.

Student surveys → atom review CS conversations → demand signal Instructor debriefs → atom annotations Enterprise requests → new atoms Usage analytics → priority ranking
